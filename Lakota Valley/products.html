Silver Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana)

Silver Sage is know by many names: White Sage, Prairie Sage, White Sagebrush, Male Sage, Cudweed Sagewort, Sage, and more.

It has used by Lakota and other Indigenous tribes across North America for centuries for ceremonial, medicinal, and dermatological purposes. The Lakota people believe that burning (smudging) of this sage will ward against maleficent spirits, purify people and places.

Smudging has been shown to purify air by removing air borne bacteria and creates a pleasant order.

Fringed Sage (Artemisia frigida)

Fringed Sage is also know as: Fringed Sagebrush, Female Sage and like Silver Sage has referred to as Prairie Sage.

Fringed Sage has been used by Lakota and other Indigenous people of North America for centuries for ceremonial, medicinal, and dermatological purposes. Like Silver Sage the Lakota people will burn (smudge) Fringed Sage for purification purposes.
